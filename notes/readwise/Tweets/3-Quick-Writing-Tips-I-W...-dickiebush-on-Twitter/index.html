# Highlights

3 quick writing tips I wish I knew 2 years ago (that would have saved me hundreds of hours):

  1. Write and edit at different times. I used to think writing and editing were the same thing – but they’re different processes. You need to give it time to breathe – so try this: • Write something • Wait 24 hours • Edit it • Send (Extra tip: edit on a different screen).
  1. Read everything you write aloud. Everything you write looks good on paper. But to notice: • Choppy bits • Wordy jargon • Run-on sentences You need to hear them rather than see them It takes a little extra time, but this is the easiest writing upgrade you can make.
  1. Write to 1 specific person to solve 1 specific problem. I used to try & write for everyone – never wanting to cut out a reader by being specific. But writing for everyone means writing for no one. So now, I practice Pinpoint Writing: https://t.co/x8hWdBRIQf
